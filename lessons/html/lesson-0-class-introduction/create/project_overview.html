Projects are a key part of this course and offer you the chance to apply everything you’ve learned in a practical, hands-on way. Each project is designed to challenge your understanding, encourage problem-solving, and help you think creatively while building real-world solutions.

1. Guided Projects

Throughout the course, you’ll work on guided projects that align with specific lessons. These projects will walk you through the development process step-by-step, reinforcing the concepts you’ve learned in class while providing a structured environment to practice your coding skills.

2. Capstone Projects

The capstone projects are larger, more comprehensive assignments where you’ll have the opportunity to showcase your skills. These projects will test your ability to independently plan, code, and execute a full project, incorporating the various web development skills you've acquired.

3. Personal Projects

In addition to the guided and capstone projects, you'll also be encouraged to start personal projects of your own. These projects allow you to explore your own ideas and interests, giving you creative freedom to experiment and build something unique.

Projects not only help you solidify your understanding but also serve as a portfolio to demonstrate your abilities to potential employers. Make sure to document your work, as each project is a valuable step in your journey to becoming a web developer.
