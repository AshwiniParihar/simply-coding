Each lesson is divided into four main sections:
Overview, Read, Write, and Create.

1. Overview

The Overview section gives you a broad understanding of the lesson's focus. It highlights the key topics and objectives you’ll be covering, giving you a roadmap of what to expect. This sets the stage for your learning and helps you mentally prepare for the content ahead. This section may also contain video content to help you understand the topics better.

2. Read

The Read section introduces new concepts and information. This part is designed to explain the core material, offering clear explanations, examples, and supporting details. It's your opportunity to absorb the theory and gather knowledge before moving on to practical applications.

3. Write

The Write section puts your knowledge into action. Here, you'll follow a guided, step-by-step process to complete a small project or exercise based on the lesson’s content. The goal is to reinforce what you’ve learned by applying it in a structured way, helping you build confidence and understanding. This will usually be done in the built in code editor to the right.

4. Create

In the Create section, you’ll take your learning a step further by working on a personal project. You’ll be given more freedom to apply your understanding creatively, designing something unique while integrating the concepts from the lesson. This is where you can add your personal touch and think beyond the instructions.

This four-part structure ensures that you first understand the material, then practice it in a guided setting, and finally apply it creatively in your own project. Each step is designed to deepen your knowledge and enhance your coding skills.
