Example: Unordered and Ordered Lists

Unordered List

Unordered lists are used when the order of items doesn't matter. For example, a shopping list:

  • Milk
  • Bread
  • Eggs
  • Cheese

Ordered List

Ordered lists are used when the order of items is important. For example, a recipe:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix the flour, sugar, and baking powder.
  3. Add the eggs and milk to the dry ingredients.
  4. Bake for 20 minutes or until golden brown.
