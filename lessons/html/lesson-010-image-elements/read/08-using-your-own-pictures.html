Why Choose the Right Images?

Images enhance your content, capture attention, and improve user experience. By using high-quality, relevant images, you can effectively convey your message and engage your audience. Always ensure that images are legally sourced and contextually appropriate.

Free and Legal Image Resources

There are many resources for free, high-quality, and legal-to-use images. Be sure to check the licensing on each site.

  • Unsplash: Free, high-quality images for commercial and personal use.
  • Pexels: Large collection of free stock images and videos.
  • Pixabay: Free images, videos, and music, with a wide variety of styles.
  • Wikimedia Commons: Media files available for educational and creative projects.
  • Google Advanced Image Search: Filter for usage rights to find legally reusable images.

Creating Your Own Images

Custom images provide a unique and personalized touch to your website. You can use tools like:

  • Canva: User-friendly design tool with customizable templates for web and social media.
  • Adobe Spark: Quick design tool for creating graphics, collages, and social posts.
  • Figma: Great for vector graphics and custom UI elements.
  • Smartphone Camera: For DIY photography, especially if you want to capture personal content.
  • Screenshots: Useful for tutorials, documentation, or showing product features.

Image Sourcing Tips

  • High Resolution: Use high-resolution images for quality. Aim for at least 72 DPI for web images.
  • Relevance: Select images that align with your message or brand. Context is key!
  • File Size: Optimize images for faster loading without compromising quality.
  • Dimensions: Use images with dimensions that suit your website layout.
  • Legal Compliance: Always check image licenses and follow usage rules.

Image Optimization for the Web

Image optimization ensures your website loads quickly and performs well. Here are some best practices:

  • Format: Use JPEG for photos, PNG for transparent images, and SVG for vector graphics.
  • Compression: Compress images to reduce file size without noticeable quality loss.
  • Responsive Design: Use srcset to load different image sizes based on screen width.
  • Alt Text: Include descriptive alt text for accessibility and SEO.

Try It Out!

Practice sourcing and adding images to your webpage. Use the example below to test an image from one of the recommended resources: 


<img src="https://example.com/your-image.jpg" alt="A descriptive caption of the image" width="300" height="200" />
Common Mistakes:
  • Forgetting to check the usage license, which could lead to copyright issues.
  • Using images with poor resolution, which can make your site look unprofessional.
  • Skipping optimization, which can slow down page load times.
  • Neglecting to provide descriptive alt text, which affects accessibility and SEO.
