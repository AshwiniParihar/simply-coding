Build Your First Itinerary Element

Now it's time to practice what you’ve learned! Follow the step-by-step instructions to create an itinerary element with various tags, including headings, lists, images, and paragraphs.

Step 1: Add a Title

Go to line 10 in your code where you see X. Replace X with an opening and closing <h1> heading with the title "3-Day Trip to Paris". 


  <h1>3-Day Trip to Paris</h1>

Step 2: Add Text Under the Title

On line 12, directly under the title, add a paragraph tag <p> with the description: “Explore the beautiful sights of Paris with this 3-day itinerary. Perfect for first-time visitors and those looking to experience the best the city has to offer!” 


<p>Explore the beautiful sights of Paris with this 3-day itinerary. Perfect for first-time visitors and those looking to experience the best the city has to offer!</p>

Step 3: Add a "Packing Essentials" Title

On line 13, below the paragraph, add a second heading tag <h2> with the text "Packing Essentials". 


<h2>Packing Essentials</h2>

Step 4: Add Packing Essentials List

On line 14, directly under the "Packing Essentials" heading, add an unordered list <ul> with list items for each essential, such as “Comfortable walking shoes,” “Portable charger,” and “Camera for capturing memories.” 


<ul>
  <li>Comfortable walking shoes</li>
  <li>Portable charger</li>
  <li>Camera for capturing memories</li>
</ul>

Step 5: Add "Daily Activities" Title

On line 15, after the packing list, add another heading tag <h2> with the text "Daily Activities". 


<h2>Daily Activities</h2>

Step 6: Add Daily Activities List

On line 16, below the "Daily Activities" heading, add an ordered list <ol> with each day’s activities, using list items for each entry. 


<ol>
  <li><strong>Day 1:</strong> Visit the Eiffel Tower.</li>
  <li><strong>Day 2:</strong> Tour the Louvre.</li>
  <li><strong>Day 3:</strong> Explore Montmartre.</li>
</ol>

Step 7: Add an Image

On line 17, add an <img> tag with the source URL: https://sc-course-materials.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/frontend-course/assets/images-global/retrotape.png. Set the alt attribute to “Iconic Paris scene.” 


<img src="https://sc-course-materials.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/frontend-course/assets/images-global/retrotape.png" alt="Iconic Paris scene" />
Tip: You may need to just copy the entire url and then paste it into the code editor. Sometimes that is easier than typing something long out. Highlight with your cursor what you want to copy. Right click or use a shortcut to copy & paste.
Copy: CTRL+C Paste: CTRL+V

Step 8: Adjust Image Dimensions

On the same line (line 17), add width="200" and height="150" to your <img> tag to set its dimensions. 


<img src="https://sc-course-materials.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/frontend-course/assets/images-global/retrotape.png" alt="Iconic Paris scene" width="200" height="150" />

Step 9: Add Final Text

On line 18, directly below the image, add a paragraph <p> with the text: "Experience the charm and beauty of Paris firsthand!" 


<p>Experience the charm and beauty of Paris firsthand!</p>

Step 10: Add Anything Unique

Get creative! Add any unique HTML element or feature below the final text, like a divider line <hr> or another image related to Paris.

Step 11: Full Code

Your full code should look like this:



  <h1>3-Day Trip to Paris</h1>
  <p>Explore the beautiful sights of Paris with this 3-day itinerary. Perfect for first-time visitors and those looking to experience the best the city has to offer!</p>
  <h2>Packing Essentials</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Comfortable walking shoes</li>
    <li>Portable charger</li>
    <li>Camera for capturing memories</li>
  </ul>
  <h2>Daily Activities</h2>
  <ol>
    <li><strong>Day 1:</strong> Visit the Eiffel Tower.</li>
    <li><strong>Day 2:</strong> Tour the Louvre.</li>
    <li><strong>Day 3:</strong> Explore Montmartre.</li>
  </ol>
  <div class="image-container">
    <img src="https://sc-course-materials.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/frontend-course/assets/images-global/retrotape.png" alt="Iconic Paris scene" width="200" height="150" />
  </div>
  <p>Experience the charm and beauty of Paris firsthand!</p>
