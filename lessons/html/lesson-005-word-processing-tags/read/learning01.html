Definition and Purpose:

  • Word processing tags in HTML are used to style text elements directly within the content. They modify the appearance of text to convey emphasis, structure, or additional meaning.
  • These tags are often used to highlight important words, phrases, or sections of text.

Basic Word Processing Tags:

  • <b>
    : Makes text bold, often used to draw attention to specific words.
  • <strong>
    : Provides strong emphasis, usually rendered as bold, but with added semantic importance for accessibility.
  • <i>
    : Italicizes text, commonly used for titles, foreign words, or emphasis.
  • <em>
    : Emphasizes text, typically rendered as italic, with additional semantic meaning.
  • <u>
    : Underlines text, often used for links or to denote special attention.
  • <s>
    : Strikes through text, indicating that something is no longer relevant.
  • <sub>
    : Subscript text, typically used for chemical formulas or mathematical expressions.
  • <sup>
    : Superscript text, often used for footnotes or mathematical exponents
